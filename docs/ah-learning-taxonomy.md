# AH Learning Taxonomy
## Educational Knowledge Base

AH tracks learning across 7 domains using 4 progression phases.
Derived from real tutoring transcripts, not theoretical frameworks.

---

## Progression Phases

| Phase | Code | Definition |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Discovery | D | No consistent response. Answers random or absent. |
| Foundation | F | Correct with concrete support (rods, dot cards, numeral cards). Cannot produce without support. |
| Retrieval | R | Correct after concrete support removed. **Never confirmed within a single session.** Requires cold recall at the start of a subsequent session. |
| Transfer | T | Applies knowledge to novel contexts without prompting. |

### F→R Transition (critical rule)
F→R Transition is not a phase. It is a transitional state.

**Definition:** Student demonstrates successful recall after concrete support is removed within the same session, but cold retrieval at the start of a subsequent session has not yet been demonstrated.

AH codes this state explicitly rather than prematurely assigning Retrieval. Intervention continues at Foundation level until cold-start confirmation at the next session.

---

## 7 Learning Domains

### Domain 1 — Make 5 and Below
Number bond combinations within 5: Make 2, Make 3, Make 4, Make 5.
Includes both commutative forms (2+3 and 3+2).

- **D:** No consistent response. Answers random or non-numerical.
- **F:** Correct combinations with concrete support. Single family stable. Two adjacent families both active → cross-family interference at retrieval level indicates Foundation not consolidated for either family. Pause progression.
- **R:** Correct after support removed. Both commutative forms accessible. Cold-start confirmation at next session required.
- **T:** Applies to novel contexts (counting on, word problems, part-whole) without prompting.

### Domain 2 — Make 6–10
Same phase structure as Domain 1.
Progression gated on confirmed Retrieval of Domain 1 across two consecutive sessions without cross-family interference.

### Domain 3 — Cardinality
Understanding that the last number counted = quantity of the set.
Includes 1:1 correspondence, conservation of number, stable counting to target quantities.

- **F:** Reports final count as quantity when asked immediately. Errors under attentional load — correctable on re-prompt.
- **R:** Consistently reports quantity without re-counting. Self-monitors and corrects without external prompt.

### Domain 4 — Subitising
Immediate visual recognition of quantity without counting, for dot patterns 1–9.

- **F:** Identifies quantities 1–5 reliably on flash presentation. 6–9 inconsistent.
- **R:** Identifies 1–9 reliably and rapidly without counting, stable across sessions.

### Domain 5 — Number Sequencing
Forward and backward counting, before/after relationships, ordering within range 0–15.

- **F:** Produces sequences with occasional errors — skips, reversals, mid-count resets. Before/after inconsistent.
- **R:** Sequences stable across full trained range. Before/after consistently correct including non-sequential probes.

### Domain 6 — Equal Groups
Repeated equal groups as precursor to multiplication.
Progression contingent on Domain 2 reaching at minimum Foundation.

### Domain 7 — Multiplicative Thinking
Multiplication as scaling and repeated addition.
Progression contingent on Domain 6 reaching Foundation.

## Interference Tracking Rules

When two fact families within Make 5 and Below are both active:

- **Interference at retrieval level with concrete support present** → Foundation not consolidated for either family. Pause progression.
- **Interference resolved within session** → F→R transition signal only. Not sufficient for R confirmation.
- **Interference absent across two consecutive sessions** → R confirmation gate cleared.

### Critical Distinction: Count-All vs Interference Error

| Signal | Count-All | Interference Error |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Answer speed | Slow — student counts | Fast — wrong answer retrieved immediately |
| Answer type | Variable | Specific — matches parallel family |
| Cause | Strategy failure | Retrieval competition failure |
| Intervention | Reduce counting dependency | Separate and consolidate families |

**Do NOT classify interference errors as Count-All.** Wrong intervention follows from wrong classification.
